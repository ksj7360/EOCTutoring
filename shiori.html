Shiori from Japan



“The classroom environment in America is so different from Japan."

Shiori is an international student at (OCC) Onondaga Community College from Japan. She came to America three months ago. She is currently working on conversational English with ESL tutors to facilitate her class participation. She stated that Japanese students rarely speak up and discuss openly about certain issues in the class. What she has experienced here in classrooms in America, nobody is afraid of speaking up and even challenging the instructors in the class. She was quite shocked.














Tutor's comment to Shiori:

Overcoming cultural barrier can be a big challenge and stressful, and especially in the school where you have to show the best result in the competitive environment. I am sympathetic as I was also an ESL student back in college. I was required to read texts written in English, submit papers in English when I had no knowledge about academic paper writing. On top of that, I could not understand what the students talked about in the class. I want to tell you though, speaking up in the class and being active is important but it is not the biggest factor to your grade to be honest. As far as your grade is concerned, your exam score, papers, and projects will be much more import than classroom participation. Plus, exams and papers are equally challenging to domestic students as well. That is to say, everybody is on the same boat. So don’t worry because they also equally have to put the same amount of effort. Second thing is that you may come to realize that getting to know a different culture can be also fun and exciting things. I encourage you go out and make some friends who speak in English. Have fun speaking in English. Weather you eat American food, play sports, visit their families watch movies make happy memories. When you do that, you will realize that your English speaking skill has improved a lot. I believe that is the most effective way to improve communicative competence. Have fun!
